MOTHER TERASA

"Peace begin with a smile"

A small timeline about Mother Terasa:

  • 26th August 1910 - Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu later known as Mother Teresa is born.
  • 1919 - Nikola Bojaxhiu (Agnes 'father) dies when Agnes is about 8-9 years old.Daid that he died of suspicious causes.
  • 1925 - Agnes is interested in helping others and missionaries.
  • 1928 (Earlier in the year) - Agnes gets a calling from God to help the poor and helpless.
  • 29th November 1928 - Agnes leaves her home for the Loreto Convent in Rathfarnham near Dublin, Ireland. The Sisters of Loreto have a missionary program in India.
  • December 1928 - Agnes has reached India.
  • January 6th 1929 - Agnes goes to Darjeeling to finish her studying and testing to become a nun.
  • May 24th 1931 - Agnes takes her first vows and changes her name to "Terasa".
  • 1938 - 1948 - Sister Teresa is assigned to the Loreto community in Eastern Calcutta (today known as Kolkata). She becomes a teacher at St. Mary’s High School. Where in 1944 she becomes the principal.
  • 1946 - Mother Teresa gets another call from God.
  • 1948 - Mother Teresa wrote a letter to the church so she could leave the convent and do Gods work. In April 1948 she got her request and it was approved by the Pope Pius XII. In December Mother Teresa started a school for the poor.
  • 7th October 1948 - The Missionaries of charity is approved.
  • 1957 - The Missionaries of charity is approved.
  • 1963 - The Missionaries of Charity Brothers is established.
  • 1965 - The Place of Peace for the lepers is opened, otherwise known as Shanti Nagar.
  • 1979 - Mother Teresa is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for Peace.
  • 1983 - In Rome, Mother Teresa suffers a heart attack.
  • 1985 - Mother Teresa receives the Medal of Freedom from the United States of America.
  • 1987 - The Missionaries of Charity start helping people with AIDS.
  • 1989 - Mother Teresa gets a heart attack. Doctors implant peacemaker.
  • 5th, September 1997 - Mother Teresa dies from cardiac arrest.
  • 19th October 2003 - Mother Teresa is beatified.

“Mother was not concerned about success, fame or power; she only belonged to Jesus. She served the poorest of the poor across the world and became the defender of the right to life for the unborn and challenged children, who were perceived as the burden to the society.”

--One of the nuns lighting candles at the grave of the Nobel Peace Prize winner on 109th birth anniverasry of Mother Terasa
